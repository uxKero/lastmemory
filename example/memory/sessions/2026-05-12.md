## Summary
Locked the core stack. Chose Supabase over Firebase for auth plus a relational
Postgres store, and MercadoPago over Stripe for LatAm payments. Created the first
schema and turned Row Level Security on for every table.

## What was done
- Created Supabase projects `orbit-dev` and `orbit-prod`.
- Wrote the first migration: `users`, `clients`, `invoices`, `invoice_items`,
  `payments`. Ran it with `supabase db push` on a branch.
- Enabled RLS on all five tables; wrote `auth.uid()` owner policies.
- Added the MercadoPago and Supabase keys to Vercel env vars (not the repo).

## Decisions made
- Supabase over Firebase, because invoicing is relational and we want RLS as the
  tenant boundary. See [[dec-0001]].
- MercadoPago over Stripe, because our users and their clients are in LatAm and
  need ARS payouts and local methods. See [[dec-0002]].

## Pending
- Build the auth screens on top of Supabase.
- Wire a MercadoPago pay link to an invoice.

## Full log
- Considered plain Postgres on a VPS with a separate auth provider, dropped it:
  too much ops for a two person team at launch.
- Considered Stripe for its DX, dropped it on ARS payout coverage in the region.
- RLS policies were fiddlier than expected; a table with RLS on and no policy is
  invisible, which briefly looked like a bug until we added the policy.
