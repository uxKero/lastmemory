## Summary
Got the first real ARS payment through MercadoPago Checkout Pro end to end. The
payment webhook arrived twice, once before the redirect returned, and our
handler marked the invoice paid twice and sent two receipts. Fixed by making the
handler idempotent.

## What was done
- Created a pay link with the MercadoPago SDK from the invoice detail view.
- Paid a test invoice with a real ARS card in production.
- Reproduced double receipt emails from duplicate webhooks.
- Added a unique constraint `(invoice_id, mp_payment_id)` on `payments`.
- Rewrote the webhook to look up the payment id and no op if already paid, all in
  one transaction.

## Decisions made
- No new ADR; this reinforced [[dec-0002]] (MercadoPago) and its known rough
  edge on webhooks.

## Scars
- MercadoPago delivers the payment webhook at least once and can reorder it.
  Handle idempotently, keyed on payment id. See [[scar-mp-webhook-race]].

## Pending
- Replay a captured webhook payload against staging to prove idempotency.
- Confirm receipts only send once under duplicate delivery.

## Full log
- First suspected our own double submit; network tab showed two distinct webhook
  POSTs from MercadoPago for the same payment id, seconds apart.
- Sandbox would not reproduce the race, which cost time; concluded sandbox timing
  does not match production and we must test with replayed real payloads.
